Lesson 16: Generating Options and Ideas to Close the Gap

Step 1: Brainstorm Specific Ideas

John begins by listing as many ideas as possible to bridge the gap. Here are some of the ideas:

  • Ask Mom for a higher allowance.
  • Save money.
  • Buy a lottery ticket.

While these ideas are not very practical, John should focus on specific and actionable ideas moving forward.

Step 2: Use the Logic Tree Framework

John applies the Logic Tree to break down his main problem:

Main Problem: "How can I buy a $500 used Apple computer in six months without borrowing money from others?"

Broader Categories

  • Increase Income
    • Money from others (e.g., ask for gifts, loans)
    • Self-generated income (e.g., side jobs, freelancing)
  • Reduce Spending
    • Entertainment (e.g., cut back on movie nights, gaming)
    • Food and beverages (e.g., meal prep, cooking at home)
    • Other (e.g., cancel unused subscriptions, reduce impulse buying)

John can now ask questions to expand the tree both vertically (additional solutions) and horizontally (more specific actions).

Step 3: Expanding the Tree

To further expand the logic tree:

  • Vertically: Ask, “Are there other ways of solving the problem?”
  • Horizontally: Ask, “Specifically, how can I implement this idea?”

By continuing this process, John will generate a variety of actionable options to close the gap.
