Step 2: Determining the Gap Between the Goal and Current Situation

John’s Current Financial Situation

Goal: $500 (cost of the computer)

Current Savings: $150

Allowance: $20/month

Dog-Walking Income: $12/month ($3/hour, 1 hour per week)

Monthly Spending: $15/month

Projected Savings in Six Months: $252

Gap: $248 (Goal: $500 - Projected Savings: $252)

Formula

Formula: Projected savings = Current savings + (Number of months × [Monthly allowance + Dog-walking income - Monthly spending])

Calculation

Calculation: 150 + 6 × [20 + 12 - 15] = 150 + 102 = 252

Outcome

John’s projected savings after six months is $252, which is $248 short of the $500 goal. John needs to find additional ways to increase his income or reduce his expenses to meet the gap within six months.
