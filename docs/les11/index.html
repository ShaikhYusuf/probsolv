Develop an Implementation Plan

Implementation Goals

  • Maximize impact: Focus on actions that raise awareness and drive attendance.
  • Leverage collaboration: Delegate tasks to make challenging actions more feasible.
  • Optimize resources: Manage limited time and budget effectively.

Step-by-Step Implementation Plan

Week 1: Build Momentum

Action #11: Change concert timing to 5 PM

Task: Notify school administration and update promotional materials.

Responsible: Eggplant.

Reason: Easy to implement and ensures audience availability.

Action #2: Announce concert on high school radio

Task: Schedule a radio slot and prepare a script including an on-air song performance.

Responsible: Tofu.

Reason: High impact and easy to implement, effectively combining awareness and appeal.

Action #4: Design posters with John Octopus

Task: Collaborate with John to create visually engaging posters and distribute them around school.

Responsible: Tofu and John.

Reason: Collaboration reduced effort, boosting visibility.

Week 2: Expand Awareness

Action #7: Boost social media presence

Task: Create posts, share band updates, and engage with students through polls and teasers.

Responsible: Eggplant.

Reason: Inexpensive and high impact, particularly for students active online.

Action #8: Distribute CDs with concert details

Task: Partner with a classmate to produce CDs; include flyers with event information.

Responsible: Miss Mushroom.

Reason: Memorable and high-impact, motivating attendance through music samples.

Action #6: Post details on message boards

Task: Write and display information across all classrooms.

Responsible: Eggplant.

Reason: Easy to implement despite low impact.

Week 3: Fine-Tune the Concert Experience

Action #12: Update setlist with new songs

Task: Choose and rehearse 20% new songs and rearrange the playlist.

Responsible: Entire band.

Reason: Keeps the concert exciting for returning attendees.

Action #13: Add jokes and stories

Task: Prepare humorous anecdotes and engaging band stories for the concert.

Responsible: Tofu.

Reason: Enhances the live experience, building a stronger connection with the audience.

Action #5: Mention concert in the school newsletter

Task: Write a brief article for the weekly newsletter.

Responsible: Miss Mushroom.

Reason: Adds a layer of promotion with minimal effort.

Week 4: Final Push

Action #1: Perform during homeroom

Task: Organize mini-concerts for select classrooms to showcase their talent.

Responsible: Entire band.

Reason: High impact despite being time-intensive.

Action #9: Create a website

Task: Work with a tech-savvy friend to design a basic site featuring event details, a countdown timer, and a music sample.

Responsible: Eggplant and tech partner.

Reason: Adds a professional touch, especially for future events.

Monitoring Progress

  • Review completed tasks every three days.
  • Address any delays or obstacles promptly.
  • Reallocate resources as needed.

Outcome of the Plan

The plan enabled Eggplant and Tofu to implement all 13 ideas without overwhelming themselves, optimize time, and leverage their network for challenging tasks like poster design and CD production.

Key Lessons from the Plan

  • Flexibility: Adjust priorities based on new insights or help from others.
  • Collaboration: Tap into the strengths of peers to simplify challenges.
  • Focus: Stick to actions that align with the primary goals of awareness and desire.
