John’s Big Dream and His First Goal: A Clear Path Forward

John’s Dream

John Octopus dreams of becoming a Hollywood CGI movie director, but he knows that achieving such a big goal requires a solid foundation. His first step is to acquire the tools to learn CGI animation—a computer. To ensure success, John needs to transform his vague dream into a clear and actionable goal.

Step 1: Setting a Clear Goal

To set an effective goal, John must answer three critical questions:

  • What does he want?
  • When does he want it?
  • What specific conditions apply?

After researching computers suitable for CGI, John decides on a $500 used Apple computer, ideal for beginners in animation. He also sets a firm deadline: he must acquire the computer within six months to start designing his family’s holiday card by December. Finally, John specifies that he does not want to borrow money or rent a computer—he intends to purchase it outright using his own resources.

John’s Goal Statement

"I want to buy a $500 used Apple computer within six months without borrowing money from others."

  • Specific: Defines the type of computer and cost.
  • Time-bound: Sets a clear six-month deadline.
  • Condition-based: Outlines how the goal will be achieved (without borrowing).

The Importance of Specific Goals

John’s initial thought, "I want a computer," lacked clarity. The refined goal, on the other hand, provides:

  • A clear target to aim for.
  • A timeframe to motivate action.
  • Defined parameters for decision-making (e.g., no borrowing).

By establishing this specific goal, John lays the groundwork for a focused action plan to achieve it.
