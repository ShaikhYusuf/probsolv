Problem Solving

Definition

Problem solving is a process of:

  • Understanding the current situation.
  • Identifying the root cause of the problem.
  • Developing an effective action plan.
  • Executing the plan until the problem is solved.

Key Characteristics

Combination of Thinking and Acting:

  • Realize the problem.
  • Identify the root cause.
  • Take actions to fix it.

Challenges:

People often skip steps or assume without analysis.

Illustrative Example: A Student’s Declining Math Grades

This example demonstrates the application of problem-solving steps to a real-world situation.

  • Common Mistakes:
  • Assumption without Action: "I need to raise my grades," but no changes made.
  • Drastic but Ineffective Actions: Quit soccer team → No improvement if root cause is study effectiveness.

Problem-Solving Approach

Step 1: Understand the Situation

Ask: "What types of questions am I getting wrong?"

  • Algebra → Improving.
  • Fractions → Flat.
  • Geometry → Declining.

Step 2: Identify the Root Cause

  • Trapezoid Area → Poor scores.
  • Cylinder Volume → Poor scores.
  • Pythagorean Theorem Application → Poor scores.

Step 3: Develop a Plan

  • Increase Study Time:
    • Wake up earlier.
    • Dedicate time before bed.
  • Improve Study Effectiveness:
    • Use better textbooks.
    • Seek help from teachers/friends.
    • Hire a tutor if needed.

Step 4: Execute and Adjust as Necessary

Act on the plan. Make adjustments based on results.

Core Insights

  • Tailored Solutions: Root causes differ for everyone. Plans must be custom-made.
  • Ask “Why” and “How” Repeatedly: Helps refine understanding and actions.

Benefits of Problem Solving

  • Break Down Complex Problems: Divide big problems into smaller, manageable ones.
  • Build Confidence: Reduces panic. Equips you to solve problems in grades, work, and personal life.
