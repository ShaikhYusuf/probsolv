Prioritize the Actions

Prioritization Criteria

  • Potential Impact:
    • How much the action would contribute to raising awareness.
    • How much the action would increase the desire to attend.
  • Ease of Implementation:
    • Time and effort required.
    • Financial or resource constraints.

Using a Prioritization Matrix

Eggplant and Tofu created a 2x2 prioritization matrix to rank their ideas:

Top Right

High Impact, Easy to Implement

Highest priority actions.

Top Left

High Impact, Hard to Implement

High priority if feasible, often requiring extra effort or collaboration.

Bottom Right

Low Impact, Easy to Implement

Medium priority actions to consider if resources allow.

Bottom Left

Low Impact, Hard to Implement

Lowest priority, often not worth pursuing.

Examples of Prioritization

Action #1: Perform during homeroom

Impact: High — Students experience the band’s music directly.

Ease: Hard — Time-consuming to set up.

Matrix Placement: Top Left.

Action #6: Put concert details on message boards

Impact: Low — Few students check regularly.

Ease: Easy — Simple to write and post.

Matrix Placement: Bottom Right.

Action #11: Change concert timing

Impact: High — Aligns with preferences.

Ease: Easy — Schedule adjustment only.

Matrix Placement: Top Right.

Prioritization Outcomes

  • Highest Priority (Top Right):
    • Action #2: Use high school radio to announce and air their song.
    • Action #11: Change concert timing.
    • Action #7: Engage on social media.
    • Action #13: Add jokes and stories during the concert.
  • Medium Priority (Top Left and Bottom Right):
    • Action #1: Perform during homeroom.
    • Action #8: Create CDs.
    • Action #4: Posters.
    • Action #12: Update setlist with new songs.
  • Lowest Priority (Bottom Left):
    • Action #6: Message boards.
    • Action #5: Newsletter.
    • Action #9: Build a website.

Adjusting Prioritization with Collaboration

Eggplant and Tofu sought help to shift some actions to higher-priority categories:

  • Posters: Delegated to John Octopus, improving ease of implementation.
  • CDs: Collaborated with a classmate for efficient production.
  • Website: Got assistance from a tech-savvy friend.

Result: A Practical Plan

Eggplant and Tofu finalized their priority actions, focusing on high-impact, easy-to-implement ideas while leveraging collaboration for challenging tasks.

Their efforts led to the successful execution of all 13 ideas!
